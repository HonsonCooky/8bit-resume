Side Projects

Neovim Proper finger placement wasn't a skill I found necessary during my academic years. With time to pause and think - improper placement rendered little impact to my physical health. Upon entering the commercial domain, the addition of dedicated architects, hybrid working, and the sheer verbosity of commercial TypeScript and C#, I saw a significant increase in keystrokes per day; Highlighting the physical health concerns of my existing typing style. After adopting proper finger placement and ergonomic keyboards, I found the keyboard-mouse transition problematic and adopted a mouse-less setup with command line interactions and keyboard shortcuts. Discovering Neovim for text-based needs, I started with community configs and later built my own. Cross-platform, VSCode-like setups were slow, so I trimmed my config to specific workloads. When attempting to learn unsupported languages, I adopted an LSP-less setup (Reddit Post) and eventually switched to Vim. Using Vim, I uncovered immense enjoyment in the requirement to understand everything that made a language viable - opting for simpler languages like Zig. However, these setups were not viable for the commercial environment I work in, and so I no longer use these tools. I spent 2 years exploring a pure keyboard-centric programming style and may return to it in the future if I find myself in an environment where deep knowledge and understanding of code are required. For now, I still practice proper finger placement, albeit, with a slower approach to allow for mouse utilization.
